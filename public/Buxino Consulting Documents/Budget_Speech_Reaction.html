Reaction to Budget Speech 2026

25 February 2026

Introduction

As a Level 1 B-BBEE, 100% Black-owned strategic services firm founded in 2021, Buxino Consulting delivers Institutional Excellence through premium corporate gifting & branding, retainer-based consulting, strategic plans, event logistics, and scalable knowledge monetization via Buxino Academy. We operate in high-ESD sectors: automotive, FMCG, TMT, and mining.

Finance Minister Enoch Godongwana’s 2026 Budget Speech, delivered on 25 February 2026, presents a cautious but pragmatic path toward fiscal consolidation and modest recovery. From our private-sector perspective — particularly as a transformation-focused SME — we offer this honest reflection: what we welcome, where we see shortcomings, and how the budget aligns (or falls short) with our mission to architect execution and drive black economic inclusion.

Key Budget Highlights – Our Summary

  • ■ Projected real GDP growth: 1.6% in 2026 (up from 1.4% in 2025), averaging 2% by 2028
  • ■ Consolidated budget deficit: 4.5% of GDP in 2025/26, narrowing to 3.1% by 2028/29
  • ■ Gross loan debt: Stabilizes at 78.9% of GDP in 2025/26; debt-service costs fall as % of revenue
  • ■ Social grants: Old age, disability & care dependency grants rise by R80 to R2 400 from April 2026
  • ■ Tax relief: Personal income tax brackets & medical credits adjusted for inflation; TFSA limit increased
  • ■ Fuel & carbon levies: Moderate increases; sin taxes up modestly
  • ■ Spending: R2.67 trillion in 2026/27; R5bn contingency for disasters; peace & security up to R291.2bn by 2028/29
  • ■ Borrowing: Reduced to R380bn in 2026 from R563bn in 2025

What Buxino Consulting Welcomes

We welcome several pragmatic measures that create breathing room for private-sector growth and ESD activity — critical for a Level 1 firm like ours.

Fiscal Consolidation

The narrowing deficit (to 3.1% by 2028/29) and debt stabilization at ~79% of GDP signal credibility to investors. Lower borrowing costs free up corporate cash flow for ESD/CSI budgets (~R20–30B annually).

Tax Relief

Inflation adjustment of tax brackets (first in three years) and TFSA increase preserve disposable income. No aggressive tax grabs protect ESD budgets, allowing clients to allocate more to transformation partners.

Social Grant Increase

R80 uplift to R2 400 for vulnerable grants supports household spending in FMCG — a core sector for our branding & event services. It aligns with our SETA youth training work.

Security Allocation

Peace & security spending rising to R291.2bn by 2028/29 addresses crime’s drag on business. Safer operating conditions benefit our event logistics and on-site delivery.

Honest Critique: Where the Budget Falls Short

While the speech is responsible, it remains conservative. Growth at 1.6% is too low to meaningfully dent 44% youth unemployment — a crisis we confront daily through SETA learners. The SRD grant stays unchanged, missing a chance to stimulate township economies where many black-owned SMEs (our potential Academy clients) operate. Fuel levy hikes, though moderate, will raise logistics costs in FMCG/mining — sectors we serve heavily. We hoped for bolder SME support: expanded ESD incentives, faster procurement reform, or dedicated youth entrepreneurship funding. Without these, transformation remains slow and uneven.

"Fiscal discipline is welcome, but growth and inclusion need bolder ambition. At 1.6%, we risk another lost decade for young South Africans."

How This Aligns With Buxino Consulting

The budget reinforces our strategic positioning. Fiscal stability and modest growth preserve ESD budgets — where our 135% Level 1 recognition delivers maximum client value. Our Trojan Horse model (gifting entry → retainers → Academy scale) thrives in this environment: blue-chips seek cost-effective transformation partners. Buxino Academy directly addresses the skills gap the budget ignores — monetising execution IP to empower black SMEs. Our SETA hybrid workforce tackles youth unemployment head-on, aligning with social grant intent but going further through real skills and jobs.

We remain optimistic: lower debt-service costs and tax relief create headroom for private-sector innovation. But we urge faster implementation of procurement reform and SME financing — the real levers for inclusive growth we serve every day.

Our Commitment Moving Forward

Buxino Consulting will continue architecting execution: helping corporates meet ESD targets efficiently, creating youth jobs through SETA learners, and scaling transformation knowledge via Buxino Academy. We invite partners — public and private — to collaborate on bolder inclusion in the post-budget landscape.
