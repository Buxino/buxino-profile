Income Statement – Detailed Justifications

2025 (Audited base year)
All figures are extracted directly from the audited cash-basis financial statements. Revenue of R5 362 came entirely from initial consulting and registration services with no physical goods sold, hence zero cost of sales and 100% gross margin. Operating expenses were minimal (R1 986 total), consisting mainly of bank charges and basic software — no salaries, rent, or marketing spend occurred due to founder-only operation. This audited low base provides the most credible starting point possible.

2026 (Post-loan ramp-up year)
Total revenue projected at R425 000 is deliberately conservative. It assumes only modest traction in the second half of the year after loan drawdown in August: approximately 5 gifting clients (average R13 000 each), 3 consulting retainers (R32 500 quarterly average), small volumes in registrations/plans/events, and early Buxino Academy sales (R100 000 across tiers). This represents a realistic ~79× increase from audited 2025 low base, driven by loan-funded marketing budget, vehicle visibility for branding, and SETA learner support enabling execution capacity — not an aggressive market-share capture or unrealistic client acquisition.

Buxino Academy Revenue Stream – Full Explanation
This new stream represents the monetization of the Trojan Horse Growth Engine™ intellectual property through Buxino Academy. It is structured in four tiers: Self-Serve Toolkit (R10 000 one-time), Done-With-You Mentorship (R35 000 package), White-Label / Licensing (R75 000+ for corporates/resellers), and Community & Recurring Subscriptions (monthly access to updates/templates).

2026 (R100 000 total): Conservative launch year — 3 Self-Serve sales (R30k), 2 Done-With-You clients (R70k), no White-Label yet (building proof-of-concept), no subscriptions (community still forming). Assumes soft launch mid-2026 after beta testing and content marketing traction. Revenue is high-margin (digital delivery + founder time).

2027 (R570 000): Momentum builds — 15 Self-Serve, 6 Done-With-You, 2 White-Label deals, small subscription base. Driven by referrals, case studies, and LinkedIn authority.

2028–2029 (R1.215m → R2.19m): Scales through network effects — more testimonials, corporate partnerships, and recurring revenue. White-Label becomes meaningful as corporates adopt branded ESD programs. Subscriptions grow steadily with low churn.

All figures are conservative: no hockey-stick growth, assumes 1–2% lead conversion, high-touch sales for premium tiers, and ESD tailwinds (135% recognition) driving demand. Academy is the long-term flywheel — turning Trojan Horse knowledge into scalable, recurring income while reinforcing brand authority.

Gross Margin Trend
